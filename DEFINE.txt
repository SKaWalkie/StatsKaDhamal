## All Important Definitions from All Slips (Easy Words)

These slips repeatedly use many statistics and probability concepts.
Here are all the definitions in simple, easy language:

---

# 1. Probability

### Definition:

Probability means the chance that something will happen.

Example:
If chance of rain is 0.7, that means 70%.

---

# 2. Random Variable

### Definition:

A random variable is a number whose value depends on chance.

Example:
Number on dice after rolling.

---

# 3. Discrete Probability Distribution

### Definition:

A table/list showing probabilities of separate countable values.

Example:
X = 0,1,2,3

---

# 4. Continuous Probability Distribution

### Definition:

Probability distribution for values in a continuous range.

Example:
Height can be 170.2 cm, 170.25 cm etc.

---

# 5. Uniform Distribution

### Definition:

All values in an interval are equally likely.

Example:
Any number between 100 and 200 has same chance.

---

# 6. Normal Distribution

### Definition:

Bell-shaped distribution where most values are near the center.

Also called Gaussian distribution.

---

# 7. Mean

### Definition:

Average value.

Formula:
[
Mean=\frac{\text{sum of values}}{\text{number of values}}
]

---

# 8. Variance

### Definition:

Measures how spread out numbers are from the mean.

Large variance = data spread far apart.

---

# 9. Standard Deviation

### Definition:

Square root of variance.

Shows average spread from mean.

---

# 10. Standard Error

### Definition:

Shows how much sample mean may vary from population mean.

Formula:
[
SE=\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt n}
]

---

# 11. Binomial Distribution

### Definition:

Used when:

* fixed number of trials
* only success/failure outcomes
* same probability each time

Example:
10 coin tosses.

---

# 12. Poisson Distribution

### Definition:

Used to count rare events in fixed time/space.

Example:
Calls arriving per minute.

---

# 13. Hypergeometric Distribution

### Definition:

Used when sampling without replacement.

Example:
Drawing cards from deck.

---

# 14. Sampling Distribution

### Definition:

Distribution of all possible sample means.

---

# 15. Central Limit Theorem (CLT)

### Definition:

For large samples, sample means become normally distributed.

Even if original data isn’t normal.

---

# 16. Confidence Interval (CI)

### Definition:

Range where true population value likely lies.

Example:
95% CI means likely correct 95% of time.

---

# 17. Confidence Level

### Definition:

How sure we are about interval estimate.

Common:
95%, 99%

---

# 18. Chi-Square Distribution

### Definition:

Used mainly for:

* variance testing
* confidence intervals for variance

---

# 19. t-Distribution

### Definition:

Used like normal distribution when sample is small.

Especially when population SD unknown.

---

# 20. Z-Score

### Definition:

How many standard deviations a value is from mean.

Formula:
[
z=\frac{x-\mu}{\sigma}
]

---

# 21. CDF (Cumulative Distribution Function)

### Definition:

Total probability up to a value.

Example:
Probability X ≤ 5.

---

# 22. PDF (Probability Density Function)

### Definition:

Function showing density for continuous variables.

---

# 23. Bayes Theorem

### Definition:

Finds revised probability after new information.

Formula:
[
P(A|B)=\frac{P(B|A)P(A)}{P(B)}
]

---

# 24. Chebyshev Theorem

### Definition:

At least:
[
1-\frac1{k^2}
]
data lies within k standard deviations.

Works for any distribution.

---

# 25. Expected Value

### Definition:

Long-run average expected result.

---

# 26. Exact Probability

### Definition:

Probability calculated using full exact formula.

---

# 27. Approximation

### Definition:

Estimated answer close to exact value.

Example:
Normal approximation to binomial.

---

# 28. Continuity Correction

### Definition:

Adjustment when converting discrete to continuous.

Example:
120 becomes 119.5

---

# 29. Combination

### Definition:

Ways to choose items without order mattering.

Formula:
[
\binom nr
]

---

# 30. Factorial

### Definition:

Product of positive integers.

Example:
[
5!=5×4×3×2×1
]

---

# 31. Lambda ((\lambda))

### Definition:

Average event rate in Poisson distribution.

---

# 32. Parameter

### Definition:

Numerical characteristic of population.

Example:
Population mean.

---

# 33. Sample

### Definition:

Small part taken from population.

---

# 34. Population

### Definition:

Entire group being studied.

---

# 35. Margin of Error

### Definition:

Maximum expected estimation error.

---

# 36. Histogram

### Definition:

Bar graph showing frequency distribution.

---

# 37. Probability Mass Function (PMF)

### Definition:

Probability function for discrete variables.

---

# 38. Probability Density Function (PDF)

### Definition:

Probability curve for continuous variables.

---

# 39. Random Sampling

### Definition:

Selecting samples by chance.

---

# 40. Independent Events

### Definition:

One event does not affect another.

Example:
Coin tosses.

---

# 41. Success Probability (p)

### Definition:

Chance of success in one trial.

---

# 42. Failure Probability (q)

### Definition:

Chance of failure.

[
q=1-p
]

---

# 43. Mean of Binomial

### Definition:

[
np
]

---

# 44. Variance of Binomial

### Definition:

[
npq
]

---

# 45. Mean of Poisson

### Definition:

Equal to lambda:
[
\lambda
]

---

# 46. Variance of Poisson

### Definition:

Also equal to lambda:
[
\lambda
]

---

# 47. Mean of Uniform Distribution

### Definition:

[
\frac{a+b}{2}
]

---

# 48. Variance of Uniform Distribution

### Definition:

[
\frac{(b-a)^2}{12}
]

---

# 49. erf() Function

### Definition:

Error function used to compute normal probabilities.

---

# 50. np.cumsum()

### Definition:

Adds values cumulatively one by one.

Used to make CDF.

---
